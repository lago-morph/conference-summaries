Bridging the Gap Between Human and Workload Identity: SPIFFE Support in Keycloak - Arndt Schwenkschuster, Defakto Security & Dmitry Telegin, Backbase UK

Human identity has been around for a while. In the beginning, authentication was just a simple password. With second-factor authentication and lately passkeys this has become much more secure but there’s always a factor of customer experience. How many factors can you prompt until the user stops?

This is different with workloads. They will endlessly provide authentication factors and won’t complain. This is great for security and building “multi-factor authentication” for workloads is easy. Keycloak supports multiple client authentication methods but many are difficult to deploy. Most of the time, simple client secret authentication is chosen. Others require key pairs and issuing self-signed JSON Web Tokens.

Since Keycloak 26.4, secure client authentication is easier than ever. Support for SPIFFE identities as a means of client authentication has been added. No more client secrets or difficult-to-deploy secret keys. You can simply use a SPIFFE identity obtained at runtime.
